# B1 — "Which hat" guardrail treats authoring and operating as mutually exclusive

## Summary
`CLAUDE.md` guardrail 1 ("Know which hat you are wearing") tells the agent to
decide which of *two* modes it is in — *authoring the artifact* or *operating
under the workflow* — as if they were mutually exclusive. But the repo's central
activity, editing `LLMbootstrap.md` (or the guardrails themselves), is *authoring
the artifact* **and** at the same time the project work the workflow is meant to
track. Because the guardrail is the implementation of **R7** (safe
self-application), this gap is a deviation from R7: it leaves a foothold for a
future session to invoke the authoring hat ("I'm only editing the product") to
skip the operating duty of reconciling against `reqs.md` first.

## Steps to reproduce
1. Be asked to change `LLMbootstrap.md`.
2. Read guardrail 1: pick *one* of the two modes.
3. Pick "authoring the artifact" (correct — the lossless guarantee doesn't apply
   to the product's content).
4. Conclude, wrongly, that the operating duties (reconcile with `reqs.md` before
   changing; keep it current after) therefore don't apply.

## Expected
The guardrail makes clear that the two modes can overlap: the *lossless guarantee*
is what splits cleanly (it never constrains the product's content), while the
*operating duties* still apply to the act of editing the product.

## Actual
The guardrail presents an exclusive choice ("decide which of two modes you are
in"), under-describing the overlap and inviting the bypass above.

## Notes / cause
The author/subject recursion means one action can wear both hats at once. The
original wording optimized for the clean case (purely authoring, or purely
operating) and didn't name the common overlapping case.
