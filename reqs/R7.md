# R7 — Safe self-application (no self-misalignment)

**Requirement.** The workflow shall be safely self-applicable: governing its own
repo without causing self-misalignment, with the author/subject recursion
explicitly guarded.

## Rationale
This project builds `LLMbootstrap.md` *and* — from the first apply onward — is
governed by it. That makes the repo both the **author** of the workflow and a
**subject** of it: the patient we operate on also becomes a manual we obey. Traces
to **G4**.

The danger — **self-misalignment** — is that a mistake in the definition silently
becomes a rule we then follow and propagate, or that the boundary between the
product and our own working records collapses.

## Design (the guardrails)
Implemented in `CLAUDE.md`:

- **Which hat** — every action is either *authoring the artifact* (normal editing;
  the lossless guarantee does not apply) or *operating under the workflow* (the
  lossless guarantee does apply). If unclear, stop and ask.
- **Product vs. records** — `LLMbootstrap.md` and everything inside it (templates,
  worked examples) is product content, never this repo's live records.
- **Don't follow a rule you suspect is wrong** — surface it; don't comply silently
  and don't edit the definition merely to justify an action.
- **The apply is deliberate** — adopted only by an explicit, user-initiated step.

## Acceptance criteria
- [x] CLAUDE.md names the recursion and the self-misalignment failure mode.
- [x] CLAUDE.md distinguishes authoring vs. operating modes and the rules of each.
- [x] CLAUDE.md fixes the product/record boundary.
- [x] The first self-apply did not ingest the file's worked examples as project facts.
